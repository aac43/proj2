PEOPLE

The following people were instrumental in the creation and design of the laserprinter with all corresponding software and GUI designs. Without the people below to change the computer publishing world, it would have taken much longer with the dot matrix way of printing. They created a major industry with computer design and started a process that we still heavly rely on today.

Steve Jobs

Clearly the most famous person on this list. Jobs founded Apple Computers and was a visionary for his time. He was the reason for heavily investing in this type of printing. He got in touch with Jonathan Seybold for advice on getting into the industry.

Jonathan Seybold

This was the founder of publishing, printing and graphics events company Seybold Seminars, was convinced that the Macintosh was the future publishing from the day Apple co-founder Steve Jobs first showed him the computer. Also introduced Paul Brainerd to Apple .

John Warnock

John Warnock had left Xerox to found Adobe Systems in order to commercialize PostScript in a laser printer they intended to market. Jobs was aware of Warnock's efforts, and on his return to California he started working on convincing Warnock to allow Apple to license PostScript for a new printer that Apple would sell. Negotiations between Apple and Adobe over the use of Postscript began in 1983 and an agreement was reached in December 1983, one month before Macintosh was announced. Jobs eventually arranged for Apple to buy $2.5 million in Adobe stock. He is also the father of Seybold.

Paul Brainerd
